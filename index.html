Krakow

Krakow is a city in the southern Poland. Krakow is the second-largest and one of the oldest cities in PolandKrakow is known for well-preserved medivalcore and Jewish quarter. Krakow was the official capital of Poland until 1596 and has traditionally been one of the leading centres of Polish academic, economic, cultural and artistic life.In 1978, the entire old town of Krakow was named a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The Main Market Square was built in the 13th century and it is the largest medival market square in Europe. St. Marys Basilica contain most elaborate Ghotic altarpiece in Europe. Wawel Castle and Wawel Cathedral are must see attractions. In 1978, Karol Wojtyla, archbishop of Krakow, was elevated to the papacy as Pope John Paul II-the first non-Italian pope in 455 years

Restaurants

1. Kogel Mogel:

Right next to the Krakow plantations, a step from the Small Market Square and St. Mary's Basilica, Kogel Mogel "turned" - a restaurant where the culinary art of Polish cuisine is shown in a different dimension. Here, where the interiors are full of embroidered Krakow obwarzanki, where painted golden-leaved chanterelles lay golden eggs, and the impressive wine cellar combined with a wonderful garden abounds in wines from the best vineyards in the world, you can taste traditional Polish dishes in new, unusual arrangements.
